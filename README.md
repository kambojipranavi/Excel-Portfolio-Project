# Customer Call Center Dashboard Project
This Excel project analyzes customer call center activities for key business insights. The dashboard visualizes important metrics including total calls, revenue generated, call duration, satisfaction ratings, and breakdowns by cities, representatives, and gender.

## Data Overview
- *Source:* The dataset consists of individual call records from a customer center, including Call Number, Customer ID, Call Duration, Representative, Date, Purchase Amount, Satisfaction Rating, Fiscal Year, Day of Week, and other relevant fields.
- *Scope:* 
  - Over 1,000 call entries across multiple months.
  - Representative and city-level breakdown (e.g., Cincinnati, Cleveland, Columbus).
  - Attributes include customer demographics, call time buckets, and rounded rating values.

## Dashboard Highlights
- *Key Metrics Cards:* 
  - Total Calls, Amount Generated, Total Duration, Average Rating, Happy Callers.
- *Call Trend Line Chart:* 
  - Visualizes monthly call volume, showing business seasonality and volume peaks/troughs.
- *Bar Charts:*
  - Gender Breakdown (Female vs. Male Callers) for each city.
  - Ratings Distribution captures customer satisfaction across service levels.
  - Calls and Amount by Weekday, showing operational patterns.
- *KPIs by Representative:*
  - Calls handled, sales generated, geographic impact, performance comparison.
- *Detailed Table:*
  - Revenue and call volume by customer and representative per city, including total aggregates for performance benchmarking.

## Insights Revealed

- Identify top-performing representatives and cities.
- Pinpoint high-value customers and call patterns.
- Understand operational bottlenecks and customer satisfaction drivers (e.g., busiest weekdays, rating distributions).
- Enable business decisions for staffing, marketing, and process improvements using compelling, interactive visual analytics.

## How to Use

1. Open Excel-portfolio-project.xlsx and go to the "Customer Centre Report" sheet.
2. Explore KPIs, trends, and detailed breakdowns.
3. Use slicers (if available) and filters to dynamically view data for cities, reps, days, and other dimensions.
